Color Overview

Color is a visual perceptual property derived from the spectrum of light interacting in the eye with the spectral sensitivities of the light receptors. Color categories and physical specifications of color are associated with objects through the wavelength of the light that is reflected, transmitted, or emitted. This reflection is governed by the object's physical properties such as light absorption, emission spectra, etc.

Color Perception

Color perception is the ability of the brain to interpret the visible spectrum of light. The human eye can differentiate between 10 million colors. The perception of color is influenced by the surrounding environment, the light source, and the individual's age

Color Season

Color theory is a practical guidance to color mixing and the visual effects of a specific color combination. It is used to create aesthetically pleasing color schemes. The traditional color wheel is a visual representation of color theory. It is divided into three categories: primary colors, secondary colors, and tertiary colors. The color wheel is used to create color schemes that are visually appealing.
